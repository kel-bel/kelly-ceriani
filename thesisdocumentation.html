Architecture Thesis Documentation

I Speak for the Shadows

The concept of my thesis is to prove the necessity of shadows within design and architecture. Shadows are the primary conductors of depth, contrast, sensory perceptions, and an impression of materiality. They define the space around us and metamorphose our awareness as day turns to night. Through larger charcoal drawing like the one above I designed several spaces studying the different aspects of shadow design.

To propose a thesis is to delve into some deeper aspect of architecture. As Louis Kahn said "...If I could think what I would do other than architecture it would be to write the new fairy tale, because from the fairy tale came the airplane, and the locomotive, and the wonderful instruments of our minds... it all came from wonder."

Without shadows design loses its ability to be spatial. To see more like the charcoal drawing above please visit here to view the full online book!
