I Speak for the Shadows

The concept of my thesis is to prove the necessity of shadows within design and architecture. Shadows are the primary conductors of depth, contrast, sensory perceptions, and an impression of materiality. They define the space around us and metamorphose our awareness as day turns to night. Through larger charcoal drawing like this one I designed several spaces studying the different aspects of shadow design.

To propose a thesis is to delve into some deeper aspect of architecture. As Louis Kahn said "...If I could think what I would do other than architecture it would be to write the new fairy tale, because from the fairy tale came the airplane, and the locomotive, and the wonderful instruments of our minds... it all came from wonder."

Without shadows any kind of design; whether architectural, user interface, or just plain sketching loses its ability to be spatial. To see the full book of work and more like the charcoal drawings seen here please visit the online publication!
